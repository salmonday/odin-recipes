Simple Scones

This sweet scone recipe will make a holiday breakfast or afternoon tea special.

An easy flour and butter based scone with raisins

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 8 tablespoons unsalted butter, frozen
  • 1/2 cup raisins (or dried currants)
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar

Steps

  1. Step 1: Preheat oven to 400F (200C); adjust oven rack to lower-middle position. Line baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. Step 2: Mix flour, 1/3 cup sugar,baking powder,salt, and baking soda in bowl. Grate butter into flour on large holes of box grater.Work in butter with fingers until mixture resembles coarse crumbs then toss in raisins.
  3. Step 3: Whisk sour cream and egg together in small bowl until combined.
  4. Step 4: Stir sour cream mixture into flour mixture using fork until large dough clumps form. Use hands to shape into ball. (Dough may seem dry at first but will come together as worked.)
  5. Step 5: Place dough on lightly floured surface and pat into 7-8 inch circle about 3/4 inch thick. Sprinkle with remaing 1 teaspoon of sugar. Use sharp knife to cut into 8 equal triangles. Place onbaking tray about 1 inch apart.
  6. Step 6: Bake scones in until golden brown--about 15-17 minutes. Cool for 5 minutes. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Variations

  • Cranberry-Orange Scones: Add generous teaspoon of fine grated orange zest to dry ingredients and substitute dried cranberries for raisins.
  • Lemon-Blueberry Scones: Add generous teaspoon of fine grated lemon zest to dry ingredients and substitute dried blueberries for raisins.
  • Cherry-Almond Scones: Add 1/2 teaspoon almond extract to sour cream mixture and substitute dried cherries for raisins.
